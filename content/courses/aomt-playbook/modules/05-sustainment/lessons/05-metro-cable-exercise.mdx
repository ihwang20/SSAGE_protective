# Metro Cable Exercise: Sustainment

Design the sustainment framework for Metro Cable's AI Skill-Match Dispatch solution after full deployment.

<Callout type="concept" title="Where You Are in the Engagement">
The AI Skill-Match Dispatch solution has completed its pilot in Zones 4, 7, and 12 with an 11-point FCR improvement. Full deployment across all 18 zones has been approved. Your job is to design the sustainment framework that keeps those gains from eroding over the next 12 months.
</Callout>

## Part 1: Identify the Drift Type

<MultipleChoice
  question="Six months after full deployment, Metro Cable's data shows that the AI recommendation acceptance rate in Zone 9 has dropped from 81% to 54% over eight weeks. No model updates have been made. What type of drift is most likely occurring?"
  options={[
    {
      id: "a",
      label: "Model drift — the AI's underlying algorithm has degraded over time.",
      correct: false,
      explanation: "Model drift causes the AI's predictions to become less accurate as data patterns shift. But the question notes no model updates were made and the drop is zone-specific and sudden — suggesting a behavioral or process change rather than model degradation."
    },
    {
      id: "b",
      label: "Process drift — coordinators in Zone 9 have developed workarounds that bypass the AI recommendation screen.",
      correct: true,
      explanation: "Correct. A zone-specific drop in acceptance rate over a short period, with no model changes, is the signature of process drift. Coordinators have likely found a way to skip the recommendation step for job types they consider routine. The fix is coaching and process reinforcement, not model retraining."
    },
    {
      id: "c",
      label: "Priority drift — leadership has stopped attending the monthly governance meetings.",
      correct: false,
      explanation: "Priority drift is real and damaging, but it manifests as delayed decisions and deprioritized monitoring cadences — not a zone-specific drop in acceptance rate. The acceptance rate data itself exists, suggesting the monitoring cadence is still running."
    },
    {
      id: "d",
      label: "Knowledge drift — new coordinators in Zone 9 were never trained on the system.",
      correct: false,
      explanation: "Knowledge drift is possible, but it would typically produce a gradual decline as new staff replace trained ones — not a sudden drop over 8 weeks. It would also affect override reason code quality, not just acceptance rate."
    }
